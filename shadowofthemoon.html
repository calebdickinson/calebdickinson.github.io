Shadow of the Moon


poem (2020)



Gleam of night, why do you trouble me?

Your knowing stare enfolds all in a deathly chill.

The wind rips through the sky.


Why in deepest thoughts you yet still haunt me?

A troubled visage darkens the shadows.

All is still — Your firmament yet resides.


Time, in its ere everlasting death

Sinks from your troubled beams of darkness.

Yet I cannot bear to see you gone.


Stars refine the creeping mist

Yet I wander knowing not the path

And seeing no direction, sleep has fled my sight.


Gleam of night, why do you trouble me?

Your knowing stare enfolds all in a deathly chill.

The wind rips through the sky.
