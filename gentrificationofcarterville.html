The Gentrification of Carterville: A Vision of Horror


flash fiction (2025)



I had a terrible vision of the future. 30 years from now, Carbondale had doubled in size, then tripled, then tripled again. A mass of urbanization spread out like a spiderweb, encompassing Carterville in its tentacles, turning it into the suburb to end all suburbs.

A chain of events had turned Carbondale into the nation's leading center for quantum computing manufacturing and research. Vast factories to the north of Carbondale produced countless quantum computers; their smokestacks bellowing fumes into the sky as scientists combined unearthly chemicals to produce bizarre forms of quantum computation.

At the same time, the climate crises of the 2030s and 40s, combined with the spectre of dried up aquifers lead to a mass exodus of Arizonans, Nevadans, and Californians. As water became nonexistent in those parts and worsening sandstorms sandblasted the Southwest, the middle and upper classes fled, leaving the less fortunate to a gristy fate.

Many fled to the new metropolis, Carbondale, and Carterville became its suburban monstrosity. Crab Orchard became a private lake, its shores filled with tasteless and structurally unstable McMansions, populated by suburban housewives from Arizona. All the buildings in Carbondale were replaced with tall 20-story corporate buildings and apartment complexes, and in Carterville all the buildings were replaced with McMansions. I saw the streets of Carterville filled with giant asymmetrical houses made of the cheapest materials, gables and turrets jutting out at odd intervals; the driveways filled with Tesla's latest vehicular monstrosities.

The cheapest rent in Carterville was now 2000 dollars a month adjusted for inflation, but in nominal terms $5000 a month. Bubble tea shops sprung up like weeds. Most locals were priced out and forced to move to places like Harrisburg.

These visions filled my soul with horror, and many more things did I see, but I shall spare you the spectre of knowledge and stop here. Alas, they say that ignorance is bliss!😭
