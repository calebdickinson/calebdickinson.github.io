A Critical Analysis of an Erotic Limerick


poem (2021)



There once was a bum boy from fagland

Who danced with the fairies in dragland

He dreamed of being a lucky Pierre

But RuPaul said he just wasn't there

So he cried as he looked on in a speedo bland


I've been studying it for my master's thesis. One analyst

Believes that lucky Pierre, being both penetrated and

Penetrating, has achieved the transcendental nirvana spoken of by

The Buddha. Yet, the poem also is bespoke

Of Byronic instability, leading one to believe of a


Pre-Raphealite influence. I think I'm going to study this poem

For my Doctorate and also write a PhD on it. THEY will understand.

It is deeper in meaning than the inspired ravings of Poe. I therefore assuage

Critics who assume an influence of Milton by defining this gem as

Having been penned in the French School of the


Early Mid-to-Late Post-Classicalist period and vein of thought.

I therefore devine this masterpiece as having been a

Towering Parnassus in the schools of Early Post-Trancendentalism.

Critics have claimed that Virgil would be unsatisfied, but I am assured that the Muse would

Consider it a testament to the Appolonian ideals of male Sapphic ideologues.
