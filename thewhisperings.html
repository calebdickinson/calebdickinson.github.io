Chapter One


It was a dark and stormy night. Snow fell slowly down into the Chicago streets as the black, swirling clouds circled overhead.

"I prefer natural fabrics" she said.

"Well of course you do, you idiot." said other woman.

Darius heard these snatches of conversation from a distance as the group slowly came closer. Something about them made his spine tingle with uneasiness.

"Did anyone hear us?" mused a male voice.

A creeping chill crept down Darius's spine. He had known many dangers throughout his life but he was mysteriously filled with a dread so intense he felt ill. He instinctively crept behind the large, green metal dumpster behind him, like a mouse hiding from view of a cat. Was it the cold and ice that made his spine recoil in fear?

"I don't think so" said the first woman.

As they came closer, Darius could more distinctly hear the more personalized aspects of the voices. The sounded elderly; austere with a hint of snobbishness.

"Salome was quite the opera." mused the first woman again, "Imagine, if you will, what it was like for the princess to kiss the severed head of Jochanaan. Quite thrilling, I suppose."

"What on earth is wrong with you" muttered the male voice, "you speak too much."

Both woman laughed. The man groaned.

The biting cold made Darius uncomfortable, and he shifted, causing a noticeable squeak as his body momentarily brushed against the metal of the dumpster's back.

"What was that sound?" said the second woman quizzically.

"What sound?" said the first.

"I heard it" said the man.

Darius heard the boots of the three crunching through the ice and snow as they walked towards him, investigating.

"I think I heard it over here" said the man.

"I don't think you heard anything" said the first woman, "you are both just paranoid. As I was saying, I think that Salome-"

"Shhhhh" whispered the first man. His voice had taken a darker tone. "I think there's someone around here. I think they heard us".

"No one heard us" laughed the first woman, "it's far too cold for anyone else to be out here".

"Be silent and listen" said the first man.

Darius felt a cold chill as the group suddenly became deadly silent. A single sound would give away his position. His back ached. Against his own will, his foot moved slightly in the snow. An almost imperceptible sound.

Several minutes passed. An eerie silence filled the place like a black cloud. The first woman broke it.

"Why didn't John the Baptist just run away from Salome?"

"How was he supposed to run?" said the second, "he was trapped in a cellar - a dungeon of King Herod. He would have been caught long before he escaped."

Darius felt the sounds of the voices come closer and more to the side of him. He silently turned his head. His fingers and toes were so cold that he could no longer feel them. The three walked passed him, so close that he could see the name of the luxury brand of their boots.

The man suddenly turned and looked directly at Darius. His face was completely expressionless. He stared for a moment, looking Darius directly in the eye.

"Sorry! Sorry if I heard you!" Darius mumbled nervously.

At the word "heard", the two women whipped their heads around and stared at Darius, their faces similarly expressionless, like haunted statues. Three pairs of eyes stared at him like a curious tiger stares at its trapped prey.

"What did you hear?" said the second women. Her voice was icy.

"Sorry! I didn't hear anything! Just something about fabrics, that's all!" he said, hoping they didn't realize he'd heard about their twisted operatic fantasies.

At the word "fabrics", the lips of the three spread apart to reveal unnaturally long teeth; not like a smile, but like the threatening stare of a wild animal.

Darius tried to run, but suddenly a strong hand grabbed his coat collar and lifted him up into the air with an unnatural strength. He turned around, gasping for air, to see the first woman staring at him, her mouth frothing with foam. She appeared to be a seventy-year-old socialite, with thin, bony arms; the forcefulness of her strength a terrifying enigma.

Darius tried to scream. As the sound began to leave his mouth, the other hand of the first woman reached around and covered his mouth.

The second woman rushed towards them and her long, knife-like fingernails pierced Darius's eyes and mangled them, leaving hollow sockets full of blood that ran down his face. As he struggled in agony, the long, knife-like fingernails moved down to his neck and began clawing at his airway, digging deep into his neck like razors.

The second woman, with both hands, strangled the writhing body of Darius with both hands around his neck, slowly crushing it as they choked him tighter and tighter. The last thing he ever heard was the three chuckling with glee.

A bright comet seen in 1880 was found to be traveling on an almost identical orbit to that of 1843, as was the subsequent Great Comet of 1882. Some astronomers suggested that perhaps they were all one comet, whose orbital period was somehow being drastically shortened at each perihelion passage, perhaps by retardation by some dense material surrounding the Sun.

An alternative suggestion was that the comets were all fragments of an earlier Sun-grazing comet. This idea was first proposed in 1880, and its plausibility was amply demonstrated when the Great Comet of 1882 broke up into several fragments after its perihelion passage. In 1888, Heinrich Kreutz published a paper showing that the comets of 1843 (C/1843 D1, the Great March Comet), 1880 (C/1880 C1, the Great Southern Comet), and 1882 (C/1882 R1, Great September Comet) were probably fragments of a giant comet that had broken up several orbits before. Amy stared at the cup in front of her that appeared to be full of hot chocolate. A tear rolled down her face. She knew what she had to do. If this cup was full of poison, she had to die first so that the children wouldn't drink it.

The whole room was quiet with an eerie silence. Amy grabbed the steaming hot chocolate and poured the whole thing down her throat, fighting to stop from choking on the burning liquid.

"Amy, are you ok?" said Will.

"Clearly not." said Mrs. Von Swofteley. "There is something the matter with her nerves. But that is something that cannot be remedied."

Amy gasped for air. Her throat and stomach were in pain. Was it the burns from the hot chocolate, or was it some kind of murderous substance that was about to corrode through her organs, and leave her gasping for air on the ground, foaming blood pouring out of her mouth and nose? The children by now seemed horrified, looking at their teacher as if waiting for a bomb to explode.

Suddenly, Will turned and looked towards the marble staircase. A young man was slowly walking down the stares, staring straight at the long table where the group sat. The lighting of the room made it impossible to see the expression on his face. Will nudged Lauren, who was sitting next to him, and she turned to look at the young man also. Just as she did, the man had walked down to the point, about a dozen feet above the level of the room, where they could both see each other clearly in the dim lighting. Lauren recognized him as the man she had pointed a gun at in her apartment, and in that same moment, he recognized her. A look of fear crossed his face, and he immediately turned ran back up the staircase.

"Oh, that's our son Lucas." laughed Mr. Von Swofteley, "He's very shy. I should have invited him down to have hot chocolate with us." At the thought of hot chocolate, he picked up one of the wooden cups and drank it all, and then asked a servant for more. Everyone breathed a sigh of relief. Lauren felt much better, although she still had first degree burns in her throat from drinking uncooled hot chocolate. Following Mr. Von Swofteley's lead, the others began to drink from their cups, and a semblance of normalcy was restored. Lauren's face was white with fear, however, and she furtively turned to look towards the staircase to see if anyone was there, only for Amy to turn her head and look at Will and Lauren as if she wanted to murder them.
